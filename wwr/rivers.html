Main Fork of the Salmon River, Idaho

Elks, deers , bears and bighorn sheeps are just a part of the splendid nature you can contemplate on the hillsides around the river! The rapids range from class I-IV. As a free flowing river the water is higher, faster, and colder in the early summer and lower, slower, and warmer in the late summer. There are good rapids all summer... but families with children who like to swim tend to appreciate the late summer's slower, warmer water more.

Desolation and Gray Canyon, Utah

This National Landmark winds through canyons in central Utah, in some of the most desolate land anywhere, the Green river flows towards the mighty Colorado river and the sea. With high grey stone cliffs, beautiful desert scenery, and great rapids, this trip boasts outstanding scenery, amazing geologic formations, and great whitewater opportunities. Desolation is rated a Class II/III and is usually done in 6 days.

Grand Canyon, Arizona

This is the grand daddy of all canyons and the ultimate river trip! You will never forget your visit to the Grand Canyon, that's for sure! Grand Canyon river rafting combines world-class whitewater with breathtaking scenery to make one truly unforgettable river experience. The canyon is filled not only with exhilarating whitewater rapids, but with side canyons and ancient indian ruins accessible only by river. The rapids in the Canyon are technically rated I - X ( 1 - 10) to accommodate such a wide variety of rapids and river variances.
